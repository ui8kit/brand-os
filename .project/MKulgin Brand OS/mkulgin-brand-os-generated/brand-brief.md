# MKulgin Brand OS — Brand Brief

Editorial brand system for a mature Russian IT operator, technical blogger, and entrepreneur focused on data, traffic defense, systems, books, and honest business practice.

## Brand Thesis

MKulgin Brand OS should feel calm, mature, practical, and editorial while communicating deep engineering credibility, honest business experience, and skepticism toward hype. Cold tech base with warm human accent.

**Promise:** Turn Maksim Kulgin's books, operating stories, and technical writing into a coherent editorial brand that is readable, credible, and framework-agnostic. Not just a blog — a tech-media hub, community club, and ecosystem showcase.

**Positioning:** Maksim Kulgin. A systematic journal on digital products, data, traffic defense, and mature IT entrepreneurship. From networks and e-commerce to startups, data, and a community of technology entrepreneurs.

**Personality:** calm, mature, editorial, practical, skeptical-of-hype, system-driven, humanly-warm

**Anti-personality (NEVER):** generic-saas-gloss, neon-cyberpunk, infobusiness-guru, startup-hype-machine, over-polished-luxury, performative-edginess

**Voice tone:** clear, measured, calm, confident, no-hype, precise, experienced, explanatory, editorial-or-conversational

**Voice avoid:** marketing jargon, motivational fluff, AI hype language, empty thought-leadership phrases, fake certainty without evidence

## Design Grammar

**Style direction:** baltic-tech-editorial

**Shape language:** Paper-like backgrounds, steel blue structural accents, restrained copper highlights, calm editorial typography, mono evidence fragments, and low-noise layouts with clear hierarchy. Network routes, index markers, and warm amber signal as the signature motif.

**Surface treatment:** Surfaces should read like an engineering journal or operator notebook, not a startup landing page or flashy creator site.
**Depth rule:** Use depth sparingly through paper layers, quote callouts, and evidence cards; avoid aggressive shadows and glossy gradients.
**Accent rule:** Baltic blue carries structure and actions. Warm amber/copper appears only as strokes: quote-bar, small labels, grants badges, chart markers — never on large solid planes.
**Contrast rule:** Long-form reading should stay soft but legible, with dark graphite text on warm paper backgrounds and clear blue link affordances.
